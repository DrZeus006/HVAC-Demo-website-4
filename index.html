FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How often should I have my AC system serviced?

We recommend scheduling preventative maintenance twice a year—once in the spring for your air conditioning system, and once in the fall for your heating system. Regular servicing ensures maximum efficiency, lower utility bills, and prevents costly emergency breakdowns.

How do I choose the right size AC for my home?

Choosing the right size depends on several factors, including your home's total square footage, ceiling height, insulation quality, local climate, and window placement. Our technicians perform a detailed load calculation (Manual J) to ensure you get a system that is perfectly sized—neither too small to cool nor too large to dehumidify.

What are some signs that my AC needs repair?

Common warning signs include weak or warm airflow from vents, strange scraping or squealing noises, unusual musty odors, frequent cycling on and off, leaks or moisture around the unit, and unexpected spikes in your monthly electric bills. If you notice any of these, it's best to schedule a diagnostic visit.

What should I do if my AC is blowing warm air?

First, check your thermostat settings to ensure it is set to "Cool" and the fan is on "Auto" rather than "On." Next, check your air filter—a heavily clogged filter can restrict airflow and freeze the evaporator coil. If the filter is clean and the unit still blows warm air, you likely have a refrigerant leak or compressor issue that requires a professional repair.

Are your technicians certified and insured?

Yes, absolutely. All of our technicians are fully licensed, EPA-certified (for handling refrigerants), and insured. We conduct background checks and drug testing, and provide ongoing technical training to ensure you receive the safest and most professional service in your home.
